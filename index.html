Key Rules

1

Awl (عول) — Proportional Reduction

When fixed shares exceed 100%, all are reduced proportionally. Base (Asl al-Masala) is raised to accommodate.

2

Radd (رد) — Return of Surplus

When fixed shares total less than 100% and no Asaba exists, surplus returns proportionally to Furud heirs (including spouses — contemporary ruling).

3

Hajb (حجب) — Exclusion

Hirman: Complete exclusion by closer relative. Nuqsan: Share reduced (e.g., mother 1/3 → 1/6 when child present).

4

${t('secAsaba')}

Sons, fathers, brothers inherit what remains after Furud fixed shares. Males get double females in same category.

5

Umariyyatain (الغراوَين)

When spouse + both parents present (no children): Mother gets 1/3 of REMAINDER after spouse's share, not 1/3 of whole estate.
